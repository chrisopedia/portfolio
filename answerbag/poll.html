    Professionally Researched. (What's this?)

    Most likely they will get rid of the 4.0 liter V8 from the current M3, to make way for a turbocharged 6 cylinder engine to power next M3, although the cylinder arrangement of the new engine is unknown, meaning it can either be arranged to form an inline 6 or a V6. An inline 6 may prove to be lighter, but V6 is more space friendly.
