Benefits of Joining the OCEA

There are several benefits to joining the Orange County Enrolled Agents. Besides saving money for education opportunities, (which is laid out nicely on the CSEA website), the OCEA is a chapter of the California Society of Enrolled Agents (CSEA)

CSEA is the preeminent society for Enrolled Agents. Through the support and efforts of our Members, the California Society is recognized as the professional organization for Enrolled Agents in California, designed exclusively to promote and protect the interests of Enrolled Agents. CSEA has an extensive public information and awareness program to educate the public about Enrolled Agents - who EAs are and what they do. It's the most aggressive media campaign ever launched on this topic. The core of our campaign is our "We SpEAk Tax" slogan used in press releases, news stories, radio and national television programs featuring Enrolled Agents. It's been a resounding success!

CSEA is one of the largest professional organizations of Enrolled Agents in the country, with affiliates and associates in nearly every state and Japan. The annual Super Seminar® is one of the largest and most respected tax education programs in the nation. Together with the National Association of Enrolled Agents, CSEA maintains an active voice in both state and federal tax legislation and has contributed directly to many of the changes to tax laws and regulations over the years.

As CSEA membership grows, the Society is able to provide more benefits and services to our Members and to the taxpayers our Members serve. Members of CSEA enjoy many benefits, such as a significant increase in the amount of available education programs, numerous discounted services, and professional networking resources. Membership in CSEA is a small investment with large returns. Members of CSEA regularly contribute to numerous publications and are sought after for their knowledge of taxes and taxation.

Membership Year

Your first year's membership is for twelve months from the date of your application. At the end of your first year, you will receive an invoice which prorates your second year's dues to the following June 30. From then on, your membership year will coincide with the Association and the Society fiscal year, July 1-June 30.

Active Membership

This type of membership is open to all persons in California possessing a valid enrollment card. Members of the Society enjoy all the benefits of membership including full voting privileges. Regular Membership includes membership in the National Association of Enrolled Agents and one of CSEA's 20 local Chapters.

Provisional Associate

Pracitioners who have applied for issuance of their permanent enrollment cards after having passed the special enrollment examination or completed IRS employment can enroll for this type of membership. Provisional Status includes membership in the National Association of Enrolled Agents and one of CSEA's 20 local Chapters, and all benefits except the right to vote or hold office. Those who join within eight months of passing the exam may be eligible for 18 months of membership for the price of 12.

  • Download Application

Professional Affiliate

Professinal Affiliation is open to all persons qualified to practice before the IRS under the provisions of Circular 230 (including attorneys, CPAs and enrolled actuaries), who are not Enrolled Agents. Professional Affiliation includes affiliation with one of CSEA's 20 local Chapters. Professional Affiliates may participate in all Member programs sponsored by the Society or by the local Chapters at rates established for Members.

  • Download Application

Enrolled Affiliate

Enrolled Agents in other states may receive CSEA benefits as Enrolled Affiliates of the Society. Enrolled Affiliates must also be Members of the National Association of Enrolled Agents and of their local state society (if available).

  • Download Application

Professional Associate

Professional Associates are California tax preparers who are lawfully engaged in the practice of tax, but not defined in §10.3(a) through (d) of Circular 230, and who have not been removed from practice under the provisions of Circular 230. Professional Associates are required to meet the same continuing professional education requirements as Members, and annually attest to the Society the fulfillment of those continuing professional education requirements. Professional Associates are also subject to all applicable provisions of CSEA's Bylaws and the National Association's Code of Ethics and Rules of Professional Conduct.

  • Download Application

Student Associates

Student Associates are students at accredited colleges, professional schools, or state ROP credentialed programs, and are enrolled in a tax, accounting, or finance career track. Individuals who are defined in §10.3(a) through (d) of Circular 230 or have been removed from practice under the provisions of Circular 230 are not eligible for this category. There are many reasons to become a Student Associate.

Other

If you do not fit into one of the categories listed above, you may still participate in CSEA educational activities and subscribe to CSEA's membership publication the California Enrolled Agent. To subscribe, email your name and address to CSEA at subscriptions@csea.org and ask for subscription information.
